# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Developers, AI builders, indie hackers, SaaS founders, and startup engineers evaluating infrastructure tools. They arrive technical and skeptical: they could build billing themselves, and they've seen a hundred SaaS landing pages. Their job to be done: monetize an AI product (subscriptions, billing, model access, usage metering, user-scoped permissions) without building backend infrastructure from scratch. They judge an infra product by the craft of its surfaces; the site must read as proof of engineering quality, not marketing.

## Product Purpose

subscribe.dev is developer infrastructure for subscriptions, billing, AI model access, usage metering, and user-scoped access control, with no backend required. This project is a redesign of the marketing website only: reposition subscribe.dev as a premium developer infrastructure product. Success looks like more documentation visits, more developer signups, more API key generation, and higher perceived product quality and integration intent.

## Brand Personality

Fast, bold, opinionated. Quiet about nothing it believes, but precise in how it says it: strong claims backed by visible product, not adjectives. Quality bar is Stripe, Vercel, Linear, Resend, Supabase, Clerk. Specifically borrow: Linear's dark precision and restrained purposeful motion, Vercel's stark minimalism and confident type, Clerk/Resend's code-first storytelling where the integration snippet is the hero.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS template**: purple gradients, identical feature card grids, stock 3D illustrations, hero-metric clichés.
- **Crypto/web3 aesthetic**: neon glows, glassmorphism-on-dark, hype futurism.
- **Corporate enterprise**: navy-and-gray, stock photography, dense jargon; nothing that smells like IBM or Oracle.
- **Indie-hacker scrappy**: emoji-heavy, meme-adjacent, Twitter-thread energy; it undermines the premium positioning.

## Design Principles

1. **The snippet is the pitch.** Code-first storytelling: show how little code it takes to ship billing and AI access. Real, runnable-looking code beats any illustration.
2. **Practice what you preach.** A site selling premium infrastructure must itself feel engineered. Every detail (typography, spacing, motion, load speed) is a trust signal to a developer audience.
3. **Show the product, not metaphors.** Interactive product previews and real UI surfaces over abstract graphics. Visitors should leave knowing exactly what the product does.
4. **Opinionated, not loud.** Bold claims, big type, decisive layout, but precision over decoration. Confidence is the absence of overselling.
5. **Speed as an aesthetic.** Fast page loads, fast decisive motion, instant interactions. Performance is part of the brand argument for "ship without backend work".

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. Maintain strong readability and contrast (≥4.5:1 body text) while preserving the premium visual design, including on dark surfaces. Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states, `prefers-reduced-motion` alternatives for all animation, and accessible form controls throughout the conversion flow.
